# 🧴 Shampoo pH Analysis & Safe Ingredient Study

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes the **pH levels and ingredient safety of commonly used shampoos**.  
Many consumers select shampoos based on brand popularity without understanding the **chemical composition and harmful ingredients** such as sulfates and parabens.

The aim of this project is to **evaluate shampoo pH balance, analyze ingredient safety, and promote healthier hair care choices.**

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement
Consumers often lack awareness about the **chemical ingredients present in shampoos**.  
Many products claim to be **natural or herbal**, but may still contain harmful chemicals.

Because of **unclear labeling and limited transparency**, users find it difficult to choose a safe shampoo for their hair and scalp.

---

## 🧪 Methodology
The project follows these steps:

1. Research harmful shampoo ingredients  
2. Study pH levels suitable for healthy hair  
3. Conduct **titration experiments on shampoo samples**  
4. Record and analyze pH observations  
5. Compare different shampoo brands  
6. Suggest safer formulations and improve consumer awareness  

---

## 🔬 Experiment
**Experiment Type:** pH Testing / Titration

Shampoo samples from popular brands were tested to determine their **acidic or basic nature**.

Hair and scalp generally prefer **slightly acidic pH (around 5 – 6)** for healthy hair cuticles.

---


## 📊 Observations
- Several commonly used shampoos were found to be **basic in nature**  
- Some products claiming herbal ingredients still showed **high pH levels**  
- **Love Beauty & Planet shampoo** showed a more **balanced acidic pH**, closer to ideal hair conditions  

---

## 📈 Result & Discussion
Very **alkaline shampoos can strip natural oils and proteins** from hair, leading to:

- Dryness  
- Hair damage  
- Weak hair structure  

Choosing shampoos with **balanced pH levels** is important for maintaining healthy hair.
